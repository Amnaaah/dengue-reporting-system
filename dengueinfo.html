What is Dengue?

Dengue is a mosquito-borne viral infection caused by aedes aegypti mosquitoes. It can lead to severe flu-like symptoms and, in serious cases, life-threatening complications like dengue hemorrhagic fever.

It is vital to stay informed and take preventive measures to protect yourself and your loved ones from dengue.

Symptoms

If you suspect dengue, look out for the following symptoms:

  • Sudden high fever
  • Severe headache and pain behind the eyes
  • Muscle and joint pain
  • Nausea, vomiting, and rash
  • Signs of bleeding (gums, nose, etc.)

If symptoms arise, consult a doctor immediately and stay hydrated.

Prevention

Here are some ways to prevent dengue:

  • Remove still water from containers, tires, and gutters.
  • Cover water storage containers like barrels and tanks.
  • Wear long-sleeved clothing to avoid mosquito bites.
  • Use insect repellents containing DEET or Picaridin.
  • Participate in local dengue prevention campaigns.

Your actions can help reduce the risk of dengue in your community.
